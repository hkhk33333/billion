Bridging the gap between being near someone and knowing someone

The Problem

Proximity Without Connection

60%

Students Socially Isolated

Over 60% of today's students report feeling socially isolated, driving rising depression and anxiety rates on campus.

50.6%

Tech Makes It Worse

Half of students say technology use contributes to isolation. Current social apps optimize for screen time, not real-world connection.

48.1%

Friendship Is Harder

Nearly half say making friends would be easier without ubiquitous technology. Digital tools replaced, rather than enabled, physical interaction.

The paradox: Students sit next to each other in libraries, dining halls, and quads every day — but have no low-friction way to discover shared interests with the person beside them. Phygtl's AR platform is the perfect bridge, but currently lacks a context-aware trigger for real-world connection.
The Solution

Spark Zones: Context-Aware Connection

How It Works

  • AR hotspot detection — When 3+ Phygtl users are co-located in a campus space, a "Spark Zone" activates in-app with an AR overlay on the physical space
  • Interest matching — AI surfaces shared interests, classes, or clubs between nearby users and generates contextual icebreaker prompts
  • Collaborative AR drops — Users in a Spark Zone can co-create AR content (stickers, messages) anchored to that location for future visitors
  • Streak & reward loops — Tokenized rewards for real-world meetups; streaks for connecting with new people weekly

Why This Fits Phygtl

  • Platform-native — Built on Phygtl's existing AR + Web3 stack; extends UGC and token systems already in production
  • Mission-aligned — Directly targets the 40% isolation reduction goal by converting digital engagement into face-to-face interaction
  • Gaming DNA — Team from Niantic, Riot, and Zynga brings proven expertise in location-based social mechanics

Competitive Moat

BeReal and Locket tried proximity, but lack AR and campus context. Niantic has AR but not campus focus. Phygtl uniquely combines AR, Web3 rewards, and a student-first mission — Spark Zones make this concrete.

Success Metrics

How We Measure Impact

⚡
Activation Rate
% of users in a Spark Zone who tap to reveal shared interests and engage with an icebreaker
In-app event tracking; target: engagement with at least 1 icebreaker per Spark Zone visit
🤝
Real-World Meetups
Number of confirmed face-to-face connections initiated through Spark Zones per week per campus
Self-reported via post-interaction survey + mutual "we met" confirmation button
📈
Isolation Reduction
Change in UCLA Loneliness Scale scores for active Spark Zone users vs. control group over one semester
Pre/post validated survey instrument; IRB-approved study with partner university

Why These Metrics

Activation measures product-market fit at the feature level. Real-world meetups validate the core thesis — that AR can bridge digital-to-physical connection. Isolation reduction ties directly to Phygtl's mission metric and provides publishable evidence for university partnerships and fundraising.
